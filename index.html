Features

Small utility. Deep where it counts.

Mind Reader is a tray-only app that does one thing — finish your sentences well — and sweats every detail to do it without ever getting in your way.

🪟

Every app, not just some

Reads native text fields through Accessibility, and falls back to a keystroke engine for Electron and web apps like Slack and Chrome.

⚡

On-device inference

Runs on Apple's built-in Foundation Models, or a local Ollama model on localhost. Your choice, your machine.

👻

Ghost text, never intrusive

The suggestion lives in a floating overlay that never touches your text — so it can't pollute undo or fight your cursor. Only Tab commits it.

◑

Learns your style

Few-shot learning from the suggestions you accept, plus an optional reference document so it sounds like you, not like a model.

⚙︎

Per-app instructions

Give it different guidance for email, chat, and notes. Each app gets its own tone on top of your global style.

🧠

Context-aware

Reads your clipboard and — if you allow it — what's on screen via on-device OCR, so suggestions fit the moment.

↔︎

Fill-in-the-middle

Looks at the text after your cursor too, so completions stay coherent with what already comes next.

🎯

Pixel-perfect overlay

Matches your field's real font size and follows you correctly across multiple monitors. No flicker, no jitter.

🌊

Streaming suggestions

Words appear in the ghost as the model thinks them, so it feels instant instead of waiting for a full sentence.

How it works

You type. It listens. You keep what fits.

1

Type anywhere

Write like you always do, in whatever app you're already in. Mind Reader watches the field you're focused on.

2

See what's next

The likely continuation appears in soft gray, right where your cursor is — predicted by a model running on your Mac.

3

Press to keep it

Tab takes a word, the backtick takes the whole line, Esc waves it off. Ignore it and it quietly disappears.

🔒 The model runs entirely on your device — Apple Foundation Models or a local Ollama. What you type is never sent anywhere.
Get started

Up and running in a couple of minutes.

What you'll need

  • ✓An Apple Silicon Mac — M1 or newer (the app is arm64).
  • ✓macOS 14 or later — Apple's on-device model needs macOS 26 + Apple Intelligence.
  • ✓A local model — Apple Foundation Models (no download), or ollama pull a small model.
  • ✓Two permissions — Accessibility & Input Monitoring, granted once in System Settings.

  1. Download & unzip

    Grab the latest MindReader.zip and move Mind Reader.app into your Applications folder.

  2. Open it the first time

    Double-click the app. macOS will say it "could not verify" Mind Reader — that's expected for a self-signed app. Click Done (not "Move to Trash"), then go to System Settings → Privacy & Security, scroll down and click Open Anyway. macOS asks once; after that it just launches.

    Comfortable in Terminal? Skip the dialog entirely with:

    xattr -dr com.apple.quarantine "/Applications/Mind Reader.app"

  3. Grant permissions

    In System Settings → Privacy & Security, enable Accessibility and Input Monitoring (and optionally Screen Recording for screen context).
